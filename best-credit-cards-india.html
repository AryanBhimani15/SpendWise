Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best credit card in India?
For premium users: HDFC Infinia Metal offers the highest reward potential (up to 16% on travel). For most users: SBI Cashback Card gives simple 5% cashback on all online spends with just ₹999 annual fee. For beginners: Amazon Pay ICICI is lifetime free and easy to get approved.
Which credit card gives the highest cashback?
SBI Cashback Card offers the highest consistent cashback at 5% on all online transactions (Amazon, Flipkart, Swiggy, Uber, etc.) with a low ₹999 annual fee. For dining and groceries, HSBC Cashback gives 10% but with lower monthly caps.
How many credit cards should I have?
Most financial experts recommend having 2-4 credit cards. This lets you optimize rewards across different categories (one for online shopping, one for bills, one for travel) without hurting your credit score. Having 5+ cards becomes difficult to manage and may raise red flags with banks.
What is the best lifetime free credit card?
IDFC First Wealth is the best lifetime free card with 2.5% flat rewards and lounge access. Amazon Pay ICICI is the best beginner LTF card with 5% Amazon cashback. Both have zero annual fee forever — no spending conditions.
Which card is best for bill payments?
Axis Ace is unbeatable for bills — 5% cashback on electricity, mobile recharge, DTH, and gas via Google Pay. Most people ignore bill payments but they add up to ₹15,000-₹30,000/year. This card alone can save you ₹750-₹1,500 annually just on utilities.
Is HDFC Infinia worth the ₹12,500 fee?
Yes, if you spend ₹3L+ annually and travel at least twice a year. The 16% reward rate on SmartBuy (flights, hotels, Apple products) means one ₹50,000 international flight booking gives you ₹8,000 in rewards — covering the fee instantly. For lower spenders, SBI Cashback or HDFC Millennia make more sense.
